01 — Our story

The birds were here first

In Thai, nok (นก) means bird — and the name is honest. Weaver birds knot their hanging nests in our old trees, sparrows line the wires at dusk, and every morning begins with song. Our small family-run hotel grew quietly around them, in a big garden of old plumeria trees, just a minute off the main road in Bang Khem, Khao Yoi.

Nothing here is in a hurry — not the lotus opening in the stone basins, not the petals drifting down from the frangipani, and hopefully not you.

The real review is the birdsong at sunrise.

02 — The garden

Four reasons to walk slowly

The garden is the whole point of staying here. Old trees, still water, busy birds — give it ten slow minutes and it gives you back the rest of the day.

The bamboo tunnel

A living tunnel of bamboo arches over the path — green light, cool shade, and the soft creak of stems when the wind moves.

Old plumeria trees

Old frangipani trees scatter white petals across the grass. They were here long before us — and smell sweetest at dusk.

Lotus basins

Lotus and water lilies bloom in stone basins between the trees — small, still ponds you find as you wander.

The resident birds

Weaver birds hang their woven nests in our trees, and every evening the wires fill up with little singing silhouettes.

Behind the stained-glass door
